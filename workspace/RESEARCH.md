## ADHD & Productivity

### Core findings
- ADHD brains are not low-dopamine by default — they're dopamine-dysregulated. Interest, urgency, challenge, and novelty activate the dopamine system when routine doesn't.
- "Time blindness" is neurological, not laziness. ADHD brains struggle to feel future time — deadlines feel abstract until they're immediate.
- Task initiation (starting) is often harder than task continuation. The first 2 minutes are the hardest.
- External accountability activates the same dopamine pathways that internal motivation fails to. An audience (even an AI) makes tasks feel more real.

### What works (evidence-backed)
- **Body doubling** — working alongside someone (or something) dramatically improves focus for ADHD brains
- **Implementation intentions** — "I will do X at Y time in Z place" is 2-3x more effective than vague goals
- **Timeboxing over to-do lists** — scheduled blocks beat lists for ADHD
- **Smallest possible next action** — "open the file" not "write the report"
- **Deadlines with consequences** — commitment devices (telling someone, betting) work when internal motivation doesn't
- **Variable reward** — novelty in check-ins keeps engagement higher than predictable ones

### What doesn't work
- Long to-do lists — overwhelming and easy to avoid
- "Just try harder" — the brain literally doesn't work that way
- Shame-based motivation — temporarily activates, long-term depletes
- Planning without doing — feels like progress, isn't

## Peak Performance (general)

### Flow state triggers
- Clear goal + immediate feedback + challenge slightly above skill = flow
- Eliminate context switching — each task switch costs 15-20 min of full focus recovery
- 90-minute ultradian rhythm — natural focus cycles. Work with them, not against.

### High performers (athletes, founders, scientists)
- They track. Elite athletes review every session. High performers in any field have feedback loops.
- Recovery is part of performance, not separate from it
- Identity > motivation — "I am someone who ships things" > "I want to ship things"
